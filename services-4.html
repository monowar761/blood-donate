AI Overview

Blood grouping is based on the presence or absence of antigens on red blood cells, and whether or not the Rh factor is present:

Antigens

The A and B antigens determine the four major blood groups: A, B, AB, and O.

Rh factor

The Rh factor is a protein that can be either present (+) or absent (â€“). This creates the eight most common blood types: A+, A-, B+, B-, O+, O-, AB+, and AB-.

Blood group characteristics

  • A: Has A antigens on red blood cells and anti-B antibodies in plasma
  • B: Has B antigens on red blood cells and anti-A antibodies in plasma
  • O: Has no antigens, but both anti-A and anti-B antibodies in plasma
  • AB: Has both A and B antigens, but no antibodies

Universal donor

O negative blood is the universal blood type because it can be safely received by anyone.

Blood transfusions

When a person needs a blood transfusion, the donated blood must match the recipient's blood.

Blood group inheritance

A person's blood group is determined by a pair of genes, one inherited from each parent.

Blood group history

Austrian-born American biologist Karl Landsteiner discovered the human ABO blood groups in 1901.
