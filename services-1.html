AI Overview

Blood storage is the process of keeping blood products at a specific temperature to ensure their safety and effectiveness for medical use. Here are some things to know about blood storage:

Temperature

Whole blood and red cells should be stored at a temperature between +2°C and +6°C. This temperature range is important for maintaining the blood's ability to carry oxygen and to minimize bacterial contamination.

Equipment

Blood bank refrigerators have built-in temperature monitoring and alarm devices to ensure the correct temperature is maintained.

Shelf life

The shelf life of blood depends on the type of blood bag used, but it's typically between 35–42 days.

Transportation

Blood should be transported in a blood transport box or insulator carrier to keep the temperature below 10°C.

Hygiene

Blood and its components should be stored and transported under hygienic conditions to avoid contamination.

Blood types

Different types of blood have different storage requirements:

  • Plasma: Plasma can be stored for up to 3 years if it's separated within 24 hours of collection and stored at temperatures colder than –25°C.
  • Platelet concentrates: Platelet concentrates are stored at +22°C ± 2°C.
